    Taupō

    This large lake located in the middle of the North Island of NZ, was once an active volcano which started erupting 300,000 years ago! Around 27,000 years ago the caldera (now Lake Taupō) was created by a super-volcanic eruption, which blew apart or collapsed the once volcano. Now a Lake, Taupō known for its stunning lakefront views, water activities like fishing and jet boating!

    Taupō has a wide range of many places to visit and fun activities for the whole family to enjoy!
    Enjoy a boat trip around the lake to visit the country famous Māori rock carving located on the side of the lake. Relax by the water and dine in and out!
    Hikes and bike trails line the lake and nearby mounts! Get on the water, in kayaks or boats!
    For the adrenaline junkies, the big sky parasail might be for you! Soring 1000ft above Lake Taupō as see the stunning lake from a bired eye view! Hukafalls Jet,
    taking you to the base of the mighty Huka Falls for the best water-level views!
    AJ Hackett Bungy Taupo, New Zealand's highest cliff-top Bungy Jump - located 47 metres (154 feet) above the Waikato River, take the plunge into or towards the crystal clear waters below.
    Taupō is a must visit! A classic kiwi town, with stunning lake views!
